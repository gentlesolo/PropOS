# PropOS Pricing Strategy

> AI-powered property operating system for real estate agents, teams, and agencies.

---

## The Core Principle

PropOS pricing is not set by cost or by what competitors charge. It is set by the value it creates.  
A listing closed faster, a lead scored before a competitor calls, a week of manual marketing work automated in minutes — that is the value. Price should always be a fraction of that outcome.

---

## Step 1 — Value Metric

**The value metric is the agent seat.**

When an agency adds agents, they get more value from PropOS. AI briefings multiply across more agents. Listings managed, leads scored, campaigns generated — all of it scales with headcount. The seat is the unit that grows when the customer wins.

Secondary usage-based layer: **AI Credits**. High-volume AI usage (mass content generation, bulk lead scoring, call intelligence transcriptions) is metered on top of seat pricing. This protects margin on heavy users while keeping headline pricing predictable for the majority.

> Never charge for inputs (integrations, time logged, portals connected). Charge for outcomes — agents closing deals with AI assistance.

---

## Step 2 — Pricing Model

**Tiered seat pricing + usage-based AI credits**

| Layer | What it is | Why |
|---|---|---|
| Seat tiers | Monthly fee per agent or per bundle | Predictable, scales with team growth |
| AI Credit top-up | Metered on high-volume consumption | Captures upside on power users without penalising typical use |
| Annual discount | 2 months free on annual commit | Improves cash flow; moves customers from monthly churn risk to annual lock-in |

Flat-rate pricing is explicitly avoided. A 1-agent solo and a 50-agent franchise get radically different value from PropOS — they should pay accordingly.

---

## Step 3 — Positioning Signal

PropOS is not a generic CRM with a property label. It is the only platform built ground-up for AI-native real estate operations — daily briefings, automated portal syndication, AI-written listing descriptions, compliance-aware deal workflows, and multi-channel campaign generation in one system.

**The price must say: we work with serious agencies.**

Pricing below R500/month per agent signals a utility tool. PropOS competes with the cost of a part-time marketing coordinator, a separate CRM licence, a portal management tool, and a compliance consultant. That combined spend routinely exceeds R8,000–R15,000/month for a mid-size agency. PropOS replaces most of it.

**Always present the Enterprise tier first.** When a prospect sees Enterprise at R9,995/month and then looks at Agency Pro at R2,999/month, Pro feels like the obvious, affordable choice. The anchor is everything.

## Step 4 — Tier Architecture

### Tier 1 — Solo `R799/month`

**Job:** Not to generate revenue. To make Agency Pro feel inevitable.

| Feature | Included |
|---|---|
| Agents | 1 |
| Active listings | Up to 15 |
| CRM & Pipeline | Basic (contacts, pipeline Kanban) |
| AI Daily Brief | 1 brief/day |
| Listing descriptions | 10 AI-generated/month |
| Marketing campaigns | Email only, 1 active campaign |
| Portal syndication | 1 portal (PropertyPro or Property24) |
| Viewings | Manual booking only |
| Compliance & Docs | Basic document store |
| Analytics | Personal scorecard only |
| AI Credits | 200 included/month |

**Upgrade trigger:** Hit 15 listings, want a second agent, or need multi-channel marketing. The ceiling is deliberately firm.

---

### Tier 2 — Agency Pro `R2,999/month` (up to 5 agents)
**Additional agents: R499/agent/month**

**Job:** Where 60–70% of customers land. Well-featured, well-priced against the Enterprise anchor above it, with a clear growth ceiling built in.

| Feature | Included |
|---|---|
| Agents | Up to 5 (R499/agent beyond 5) |
| Active listings | Unlimited |
| CRM & Pipeline | Full (buyer matching, deal scoring, automation) |
| AI Daily Brief | Full brief + nudges for all agents |
| Listing descriptions | Unlimited AI-generated |
| Marketing campaigns | All channels (Instagram, Facebook, LinkedIn, WhatsApp, Email, SMS, Portal ads) |
| Portal syndication | Both portals (PropertyPro + Property24) |
| Viewings | Self-service booking, route optimisation, automated reminders, feedback collection |
| Compliance & Docs | Full FICA compliance, attorney portal, commission management |
| Analytics | Agency dashboard, listing health, revenue forecasting |
| Training Hub | Onboarding flows, skills library, AI objection handler |
| AI Credits | 2,000 included/month |

**Upgrade trigger:** Exceed 5 agents, need multi-branch management, require white-label, or need API access for custom integrations. The seat cap makes the upgrade conversation happen organically.

---

### Tier 3 — Enterprise `Custom — Talk to us`

**Job:** Make Agency Pro feel safe and affordable by comparison. Also capture large franchise groups and multi-branch agencies at deal sizes that justify custom packaging.

Do not publish a public price. A public Enterprise price caps the conversation upward and leaves 60–70% of contract value on the table. Every enterprise deal is custom.

| Feature | Included |
|---|---|
| Agents | Unlimited |
| Branches | Multi-branch management dashboard |
| White-label | Custom branding, domain, login screen |
| AI Model selection | Choose GPT-4o, DeepSeek, or bring your own key |
| API access | Full REST API for custom integrations |
| SLA | 99.9% uptime guarantee, dedicated support channel |
| Onboarding | Dedicated implementation manager |
| Compliance | Custom compliance workflows, bulk FICA |
| AI Credits | Custom allocation + committed minimums |
| Billing | Annual invoicing, custom payment terms |

Minimum commitment: R9,995/month. Typical deal range: R12,000–R40,000/month for franchise groups.

## Step 5 — AI Credits Explained

AI Credits are consumed by high-intensity operations:

| Action | Credit cost |
|---|---|
| AI listing description | 5 credits |
| Full campaign generation (all channels) | 20 credits |
| Lead scoring batch (per 50 leads) | 10 credits |
| Call intelligence transcription (per call) | 15 credits |
| Daily brief generation | 2 credits |
| Buyer-listing match analysis | 5 credits |

Solo agents (200 credits/month) comfortably cover standard daily use. Agency Pro agents (2,000 credits/month across 5 agents) covers heavy usage. Power users who exceed their allocation are offered top-up packs:

| Pack | Credits | Price |
|---|---|---|
| Starter top-up | 500 | R199 |
| Pro top-up | 2,000 | R599 |
| Bulk | 10,000 | R2,499 |

## Step 6 — Annual Commit Discount

Annual billing should become the default offer, not an afterthought.

| Plan | Monthly | Annual (2 months free) | Saving |
|---|---|---|---|
| Solo | R799/month | R7,990/year (R666/month equiv.) | R1,598 |
| Agency Pro | R2,999/month | R29,990/year (R2,499/month equiv.) | R5,998 |

Introduce annual billing in the first sales conversation, not as a retention save. Phrase it as: *"Most agencies lock in annual to get two months free and guarantee their rate while we grow."* This reframes it from a discount ask to a savvy business decision.

## Step 7 — Self-Hosted Licence Pricing

For agencies that require on-premise deployment (data sovereignty, large enterprise IT requirements):

| Tier | Annual Licence | What's included |
|---|---|---|
| Self-Hosted Pro | R49,990/year | Full Agency Pro features, 1 branch, licence key, 12 months updates |
| Self-Hosted Enterprise | From R149,990/year | Unlimited agents, multi-branch, white-label, custom AI key, priority support |

Self-hosted licences do not include AI Credits — customers supply their own API keys. Update renewals: 50% of licence fee/year.

## Step 8 — Upgrade Triggers (Build Them In)

The best upgrade conversations happen because the product hit a natural ceiling, not because a sales rep called.

| Trigger | Which tier | Where it surfaces |
|---|---|---|
| Listing count exceeds 15 | Solo → Pro | In-app banner at 13 listings |
| Second agent added | Solo → Pro | On agent invite attempt |
| Third portal connection attempted | Solo → Pro | On portal settings page |
| Agent count exceeds 5 | Pro → Enterprise | On agent invite at seat 6 |
| Multi-branch creation attempted | Pro → Enterprise | On branch creation page |
| AI Credit balance drops below 20% | Any | Persistent dashboard alert with top-up or upgrade CTA |

Each trigger should surface a contextual upgrade prompt that shows the cost difference and the immediate feature unlocked — not a generic "upgrade now" modal.

---

## Step 9 — Willingness to Pay Research

Before finalising numbers, run Van Westendorp surveys with 20+ active users and prospects across three segments:

1. Solo agents (1 person operations)
2. Small agencies (2–10 agents)
3. Mid-size agencies (10–50 agents)

Key questions:
- *"At what monthly price would PropOS feel so cheap you'd question the quality?"*
- *"At what price would PropOS feel like a bargain?"*
- *"At what price would PropOS start to feel expensive?"*
- *"At what price would PropOS be too expensive to consider?"*

The intersections of those four curves define the acceptable pricing range. Aim to land the Pro tier in the upper half of the acceptable range — not the midpoint. Founders consistently undercharge. The data usually confirms a price 20–40% higher than initial instinct.

---

## Step 10 — Pricing Review Cadence

Pricing is not set once. It is iterated.

| Review | Frequency | What to check |
|---|---|---|
| Conversion by tier | Monthly | Are customers landing where expected? (60–70% on Pro) |
| Churn by tier | Monthly | Is any tier showing disproportionate churn? |
| Expansion revenue | Monthly | Are customers upgrading? At what trigger? |
| Willingness to pay | Quarterly | Are win/loss interviews revealing price sensitivity? |
| Competitor movements | Quarterly | Has anyone repositioned up or down? |
| Full pricing review | Annually | Consider price increases anchored to value delivered |

A 1% improvement in pricing delivers an 8% improvement in operating profit (McKinsey, S&P 1500). Monthly pricing reviews are not optional — they are the highest-ROI activity in the business after product development.

---

## Pricing Maturity Checklist

- [ ] Value metric confirmed as agent seat (revisit if usage patterns suggest otherwise)
- [ ] Customer segments clearly defined (Solo, Agency, Enterprise)
- [ ] Van Westendorp surveys completed with 20+ participants per segment
- [ ] Tier feature allocation mapped on value × adoption matrix
- [ ] Upgrade triggers built into product UI
- [ ] Annual billing introduced as default in sales motion
- [ ] AI Credits consumption tracked per feature to verify credit pack pricing
- [ ] Pricing reviewed monthly, not annually
- [ ] Self-hosted licence pricing validated with at least 3 enterprise prospects
